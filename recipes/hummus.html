Lebanese Hummus

So, what is Hummus after all?

So, the heavenly dip you get for a couple of buck at the supermarket, hummus (also spelled houmous, hommos, or hummous, by the way) is a creamy dip or spread made from mashed chickpeas, fresh lemon juice, tahini (sesame seed paste), and garlic.

Interestingly, "hummus" is Arabic for "chickpea", الحمص , which I didn't know. Makes sense, huh?

Ingredients

  • A can of chickpeas
  • Tahini (1 tbsp)
  • Salt (1 tsp)
  • Lemon juice (as liked)
  • One garlic clove (optional)

Steps

  1. Begin by draining and rinsing the chickpeas and then adding them to a pot, cover with water.
  2. In a food processor, add the tender chickpeas, lemon juice, tahini, garlic and salt.
  3. Blend until smooth and creamy. Taste and adjust the salt, lemon and tahini if necessary.
  4. Transfer the hummus to a serving dish, and drizzle it with (optional) extra virgin olive oil and sprinkle with sumac, paprika or cumin.

Enjoy!
